Vibe Coding Spaghetti Bowl

Exploring the human-driven, intuitive side of software development and its risks.

This website summarizes Myra Roldan’s LinkedIn article describing the phenomenon known as “vibe coding”: the tendency for developers to rely on intuition and aesthetics when building software — often at the cost of sustainable architecture. Through a cautionary client story, the article highlights the dangers of rapid, intuition-driven app creation and the critical need for planning, security, and architectural discipline.

Vibe coding feels like magic. No code, no waiting on developers. Just you, your idea, and a tool promising drag-and-drop simplicity for rapid creation. But beneath that magic lies risk: broken APIs, security holes, exposed code, and unreliable apps. This story exposes the dark side no one talks about.

How It Starts: The High of Building Fast

The allure of no-code and AI-assisted tools feels enchanting. Developers and entrepreneurs see their ideas materialize within minutes, bypassing traditional development cycles. This dopamine rush fuels rapid progress and empowerment.

However, when speed is the sole focus, foundational questions get ignored:

  • What problem is being solved?
  • What does the user journey look like?
  • How will data flow through the system?
  • Can it scale from 10 to 100 users gracefully?
  • How are security and code maintainability addressed?

Without introspection, feature bloat ensues, leading to fragile systems built on shaky assumptions.

The Cracks in the System

Partial functionality often masks deeper problems. The client’s app worked “kind of,” but unseen flaws compromised reliability, scalability, and security.

  • Scalability stalls: Performance degraded rapidly with user growth.
  • Spaghetti workflows: Unstructured logic piled without clear architecture.
  • Single points of failure: Critical undocumented components threatened entire operation.
  • Security gaps: Permissions and sensitive info were dangerously lax.
  • Integration chaos: Updates unpredictably broke chained processes.

The Illusion of a “Super App”

The desire to create an all-in-one solution is widespread, but monolithic “super apps” built without modular design are fragile. Traditional engineering emphasizes decomposition into smaller, manageable components to safeguard stability.

The client’s super app became a single point of failure — a crash meant business stoppage.

The Lessons No One Wants to Hear

Vibe coding itself isn’t the problem — neglecting fundamental software engineering principles is. Before coding, the following are essential:

  • Clearly define the problem.
  • Create a system architecture blueprint.
  • Plan for scalability and fault tolerance.
  • Map security policies and governance upfront.
  • Conduct “what if” scenarios for resilience.

The Clean-Up Costs More Than the Build

Post-mortem fixes for haphazard vibe-coded projects incur heavy costs — rebuilding workflows, migrating inconsistent data, patching security flaws, staff retraining, and documentation creation.

A solid, well-planned solution is faster and cheaper upfront than cleaning a mess cut loose by haste.

Why This Keeps Happening

Vibe coding democratizes creation, empowering non-traditional builders, which is positive. However, the illusion that “fast = sustainable” leads to oversights in architecture and governance.

Misplaced confidence, not bad intent, causes fragile systems to emerge repeatedly.

What I Tell Clients

I don’t recommend starting over — creativity and speed are strengths. However, clients must:

  • Preserve their creative spark.
  • Incorporate fundamental software discipline.
  • Build modularly, in phases.
  • Plan exit/migration strategies for platforms.

The Bigger Picture

Vibe coding changes who gets to build software, which is revolutionary. But without reckoning with risks, many will build on unstable ground.

True innovation balances speed with durability. Pause to plan before the first drag and drop — or prepare for costly cleanups later.
